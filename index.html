PIX 2013.

A fun, active, hands on approach to encouraging kids to explore photography. We’re going to build on the experiences of previous years and mix other media with our digital photography. This workshop has been designed for children from ages five to twelve; from those who already own/use a digital camera to those who have absolutely no experience. It will even be rewarding for children who have previously preferred painting, drawing and have no photographic experience.

Photo-walks. Making negatives. Learning digital processing. Solar printing. Making a giant Camera Obscura. Pin hole cameras. Learning photgraphic skills. Mug Shots and the history of portraiture. What do we do? It'll blow your mind.

We cover everything, from the basics on how to treat your camera, and ensuring that everyone knows how it works, to composition, training ourselves to notice the best shot and capturing subjects from various viewpoints. We'll have "treasure hunt" photo-walks, shoot portraits, landscapes and environmental art. We'll use sunshine to print photographs on to wood. We'll even discover some of the stranger stories from the history of photography and learn some basic photo processing. We learn to make pinhole cameras, and turn a house into a camera obscura. There are around four children to a group. Keeping the workshop size small means that everyone gets plenty of individual attention.

What do you bring? Enthusiasm Creativity.

Child’s own digital camera or borrow mum or dad’s camera (compact point and shoot, or DSLR), an empty memory card, (we don’t want to start out sorting through all your old holiday pictures, and you don’t want that either) a fully charged camera battery. We have a limited number of cameras that children can borrow for the workshop if they forget their own, however it is preferable that they do bring their own, for continuity over all sessions.Everyone will have a get an opportunity to use a DSLR camera during the week. If you're staying for a full day bring lunch too.

What do you take home?

Some large photographs, and mixed media pieces, including a 8x8 or 8x10 solar ink print on wood.The confidence that you are the most knowledgable and skilled photographer in the family.
